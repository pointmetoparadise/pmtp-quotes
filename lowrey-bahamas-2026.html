Sandals Royal Bahamian

historic Cable Beach club with a private offshore island
HONEST FIT CHECK — READ THIS FIRST Sandals is designed and marketed for couples — not just adults-only, but specifically two-people-per-room, romance-forward. Pairs of friends or family members 18+ are welcome, but the rooms in this category are king-bed configurations (no twin or two-bed setup), and the resort atmosphere leans heavily into candlelit beach dinners, fire pits, and couples spa rooms. If the four of you are two couples, this is a perfect match. If you'd want twin or separate-bed setups, the room config won't work — see Atlantis or the alternatives below.

The setup is two-part: the main resort sits on Cable Beach about fifteen minutes from the airport, and Sandals' own offshore island, Barefoot Cay, is a short complimentary boat ride away. The cay has two beaches (one for lounging, one with the resort's better snorkeling), a Zen garden, swim-up bars, and food trucks running Bahamian conch and fresh crepes. Most guests end up splitting their days between the two — the boat back at sunset is one of the small details people remember.

On the main property: ten restaurants, eight bars (three swim-up), seven pools and five whirlpools. Dining moves through La Plume's classic French, Tesoro for Italian, Butch's Island Chop House for steaks, Kimono's teppanyaki, Soy Sushi Bar, the Queen's Pearl pub, and Kanoo for pan-Caribbean. Aralia House is the seafood spot out on the cay. The Red Lane Spa is on-site, and scuba is included for certified divers — uncommon in the all-inclusive category and a real perk if anyone in the party dives.

This is Cable Beach, not Paradise Island. Cable Beach is the older, quieter strip — closer to downtown Nassau, with Baha Mar's casino next door if you want it, and a calmer end-of-day energy than the Atlantis side has.

The Royal at Atlantis

iconic Paradise Island towers · Aquaventure included

The Royal is the iconic pink-towered hotel at the center of the Atlantis complex on Paradise Island — the building you've seen on every Atlantis ad since 1998. The lobby actually opens onto The Dig, the world's largest open-air marine habitat, so you walk past sharks and rays on the way to the elevator. It's the kind of detail teens love and adults stop pretending they don't.

Stay-included access to Aquaventure (the 141-acre water park), all property pools, the marine habitats, and the Atlantis Casino. Twenty-one restaurants on property — Nobu and Paranza by Michael White at the high end, Carmine's family-style Italian, Cafe Martinique, and Marina Village's casual Bahamian options at the other. Cabbage Beach is the long stretch of white sand out back.

Why The Royal over the other Atlantis towers The Cove is the upscale, more secluded option — beautiful, but most teens find it boring. The Reef and The Beach offer residential-style rooms, but they're last on the renovation list, so they don't feel as fresh. The Royal sits in the middle of the action, just refashioned in a $150 million renovation completed in 2024, and it's where the celebration energy lives.

The trade against Sandals: Atlantis is bigger, louder, and pricing is unbundled. Food, drinks, gratuities, and a daily resort fee come on top of the room rate. The upside is Aquaventure access included, the casino-and-aquarium energy, and one shared family-style room that puts everyone under one roof.

Hotel Riu Palace Paradise Island

all-inclusive on Cabbage Beach, right next to Atlantis

Riu Palace sits directly on Cabbage Beach next door to Atlantis — same long stretch of white sand, but at a more relaxed adults-only price point. The all-inclusive covers all meals, drinks, room service, and the four à-la-carte restaurants (Krystal Fusion, Tengoku Japanese, Sir Alexander French, Bahamas Steakhouse) plus the main Atlantic buffet. Two pools, swim-up bar, Renova Spa, beach access. 379 rooms in a single tall building, well-maintained. Family Suites are available with two-bedroom configurations if separate sleeping arrangements matter.

HONEST READ Riu Palace is a 4-star property — comfortably above budget, comfortably below Sandals' polish. Reviews flag aging décor in some rooms, vendor pressure on the public beach, and occasional rowdy adult-only-resort energy that won't suit everyone. Service is hit or miss. Best fit if budget is the deciding factor and you want a fun, relaxed adults-only beach scene without Sandals' romance focus or Atlantis's à-la-carte dining math.

Option 4 · all-inclusive · alternative Adults-only · 16+

Warwick Paradise Island Bahamas

smaller, calmer adults-only on the Nassau Harbour side

Warwick is the smaller, more boutique adults-only on Paradise Island — 250 rooms in a tower overlooking Nassau Harbour, on the south shore. Five restaurants (Verandah buffet, Abbiocco Italian, Tings on a Stick pan-Asian, Edgewater Grill steakhouse over water, plus Chickcharnie's poolside grill), two bars, Amber Spa, lagoon pool and lap pool. All-inclusive covers tips and taxes. Strong scores for staff service and value.

HONEST READ Warwick scores well on staff and value (8.0 on Booking.com, ranks well on TripAdvisor) but it's not directly on Cabbage Beach — the property sits on the Nassau Harbour side with a small enclosed swimming area; Cabbage Beach is a short shuttle or 10-minute walk. Food reviews are mixed, and rooms are modestly appointed. Best fit if you want a calmer, more intimate property with attentive service and you're OK with a harbour-side rather than beach-side setting.
