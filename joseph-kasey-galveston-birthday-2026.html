Guarantee vs. Pick Your Own — The room itself is the same balcony category either way. With the Guarantee, Royal Caribbean assigns the specific stateroom (you find out closer to sailing) and you save $589. With the Pick Your Own, you see the deck plan and choose your exact cabin — useful if you want a specific deck, location on the ship, or to be near something specific.
⚑ One thing to know about the Guarantee — Because Royal Caribbean picks the cabin, there's a small chance of being placed in a balcony stateroom with a partially obstructed view (a lifeboat below the balcony, a column or overhang nearby). Choose your cabin experience
The three MSC experiences, briefly — MSC bundles the cabin class with the onboard experience, so each option is a different version of the cruise, not just a different room.

Bella (Option A) — Entry tier. Standard balcony, but MSC assigns the cabin (you won't know deck or location until close to sailing), fixed dining time, and there's a small chance of getting a partially obstructed view.

Fantastica (Option B) — The middle tier and, at just $16 more than Bella, the smart value pick. You get the same deluxe balcony category as Aurea (172–183 sq ft on decks 9–10), you pick the exact cabin location (which removes the obstructed-view risk entirely), continental breakfast delivered to the cabin, 24-hour room service access, and a specialty dining pre-cruise discount. For sixteen dollars, Fantastica turns a roll-of-the-dice guarantee into a controlled, well-located balcony.

Aurea (Option C) — Premium experience tier. Everything Fantastica includes, plus:
  • Complimentary access to the Aurea Spa Thermal Suite — heated loungers, hydrotherapy pools, sauna, steam
  • Welcome prosecco and chocolate in the cabin on arrival
  • "Dine any time" flexible dining (no fixed seating)
  • Access to the Top Exclusive Solarium — an Aurea-only sun deck
  • Priority boarding and priority luggage drop-off
  • 10% off onboard spa treatments
  • Cabin can be booked up to deck 15 (vs. Fantastica's decks 9–10)

My honest take for Kasey's birthday:
  • Bella doesn't really make sense here. Fantastica is only $16 more and solves the guarantee-risk problem.
  • Fantastica is the smart pick if budget is the main driver. Same balcony category as Aurea, you pick the cabin, breakfast in bed.
  • Aurea is the celebration-shaped pick. The thermal suite, prosecco welcome, flexible dining, and quieter solarium are the details that turn a standard cruise into a birthday-right trip. My recommendation.

Note: Aurea no longer includes a drinks package or Wi-Fi — those were bundled years ago but are now priced separately. The three optional add-ons below let you layer those in if you want them.
⚑ One thing to know about Bella — Because MSC assigns the cabin on a Bella guarantee, there's a small chance of ending up in a balcony stateroom with a partially obstructed view (a lifeboat below, a column nearby, that kind of thing). It's not common, but it's possible. Stepping up to Fantastica (just +$16) or Aurea lets you pick the exact cabin before booking, so this risk goes away entirely.
