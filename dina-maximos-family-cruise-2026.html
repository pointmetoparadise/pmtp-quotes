How to Read Your Cabin Options

✿ ✿ ✿

Guarantee vs. Assigned cabins

Royal Caribbean sells cabins two ways, and you'll see both in your options below.

Assigned (categories 3V, 4B, SB) — You book a specific cabin number on a specific deck. You know exactly where you're sleeping when you book. More control, slightly higher price.

Guarantee (categories ZQ, XB) — You book the category, and Royal assigns your specific cabin closer to sailing — sometimes the day before. The cabin will be in that category or higher. Lower fare in exchange for less control over location.

Why are there four kinds of balcony?

  • Standard Ocean View Balcony — 198 sq ft cabin, 42 sq ft balcony, side of the ship. The default balcony.
  • Spacious Ocean View Balcony (4B) — 203 sq ft cabin, 42 sq ft balcony. Five extra square feet plus a sofa — meaningful if four people are sharing the space.
  • Sunset Ocean View Balcony (SB) — 198 sq ft cabin, 46 sq ft balcony, at the back of the ship. You're looking straight down the wake. Dramatic and quiet at sea, but a longer walk to midship venues.
  • Quad Balcony Guarantee (XB) — A balcony cabin that sleeps four, category assigned by Royal closer to sailing.

Interior vs. Balcony — the honest trade

Interior cabins are windowless. Fully dark when the lights go off, which some people sleep better in. They save you roughly $400–$1,000 over a balcony depending on the category.

Balcony cabins add daylight, outdoor space, and (right now) a free soda package for two as a Royal Caribbean promotion. On a 4-night cruise with two sea days, the balcony actually gets used. On a port-heavy itinerary where you're off the ship most days, interior is more defensible.

Group leader bonus — Royal Caribbean

Earn $50–$75 toward your own cruise for every cabin booked

As the group leader, you earn $50–$75 per additional cabin booked under your group, applied directly toward your own fare. Four additional couples booking with you means $200–$300 back on your cabin. The exact amount depends on which categories the additional travelers pick.

How Carnival Breeze differs from Mariner of the Seas

Carnival Breeze is a Dream-class ship — slightly larger than Mariner (around 3,600 guests vs 3,300). Refurbished in 2022. Onboard culture runs more casual and lively: Guy Fieri's burgers, BlueIguana taco bar, RedFrog Rum Bar, Punchliner Comedy Club, an adults-only Serenity retreat on the top deck, and the WaterWorks aqua park anchored by a 320-foot Twister waterslide. Camp Ocean (kids), Circle 'C' (tweens), and Club O2 (teens) cover the youth programs.

Mariner of the Seas leans a touch more polished — specialty dining at Jamie Oliver's Italian and Izumi Sushi, the FlowRider surf simulator (which Carnival Breeze doesn't have), Battle for Planet Z laser tag, and an ice-skating rink. Both ships work well for family groups — the choice often comes down to which line you've sailed before and which onboard culture fits the trip.

One pricing note: Royal's current balcony promotion includes a free soda package for two guests, which is a real-dollar value Carnival isn't bundling right now. If you go Carnival, drinks and packages are added on separately.
