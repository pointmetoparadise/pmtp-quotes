Understanding your options

Bella vs. Fantastica vs. Aurea

MSC sells cruises in three "experience" tiers (plus the Yacht Club, which is a separate ship-within-a-ship concept and not part of this quote). All three include your cabin, all main meals, entertainment, and access to the ship's pools, gym, and amenities. The differences are about where your cabin is, how flexible your dining is, and what extras you get on board.

Important distinction: In the US market, none of these experiences include a drinks package or WiFi. Those are separate add-ons — which is why you see two price columns in this quote ("Cruise Only" vs. "Drinks & WiFi Included"). You pick your experience level first, then decide if you want to add drinks/WiFi on top.

The honest take for a couples group

Bella (lowest fare): You still get a balcony cabin — that part is the same. What you give up is the ability to pick your cabin's deck or position. MSC assigns it closer to sailing. Works fine if you don't care where you land and you're spending most of your time off exploring ports and the ship anyway.

Fantastica (mid-tier): The sweet spot for most cruisers. You pick your deck and cabin, you get free room service, and you can request an early or late dining time. Worth the small bump up from Bella for the cabin choice alone — especially for a couples group where keeping everyone close matters.

Aurea (premium tier): For a couples group, Aurea actually makes a lot of sense. The real value is two things — the thermal suite and the Top Exclusive Solarium on Deck 19. The solarium is adults-only, has a private bar, hot tubs, loungers, and rarely gets crowded because access is limited. If your group wants a quieter, adults-only space to unwind — no kids, no family-cruise bustle — that's what Aurea gets you. Plus My Choice dining means you're not locked to a 5:30 or 8:00 seating time — couples can come down to dinner whenever suits the day.

What Aurea does NOT include in the US: drinks and WiFi. Those are separate. Don't pay for Aurea expecting all-inclusive — pay for Aurea if the spa, solarium, and dining flexibility matter to your group.
