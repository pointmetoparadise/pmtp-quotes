Your Ship

Icon of the Seas, in plain English

Royal Caribbean's flagship, in service since January 2024. The world's largest cruise ship by gross tonnage at launch — and built around the idea of eight distinct neighborhoods so it doesn't feel like one giant crowd.

Surfside

Where your family will spend the most time.

The family-focused neighborhood, on Decks 7–8. Splashaway Bay (kids' splash pad and slides), Surfside Eatery (casual all-day dining), Pier 7 carousel, mini-golf, and a dedicated family pool. Everything sized and themed for young kids.

Thrill Island

For the adrenaline crew.

Home of Category 6, the largest waterpark at sea, with six slides. Also the FlowRider surf simulator and Crown's Edge skywalk. Older kids and teens gravitate here.

Chill Island

Sun, pools, slow afternoons.

Three pools across this neighborhood including the largest pool at sea, plus Swim & Tonic — the longest swim-up bar at sea. The relaxed, lay-flat side of the ship.

Central Park

Open-air dining hub.

An open-air park in the middle of the ship with real plants. Most of the upscale dining sits here — Chops Grille, Giovanni's Italian Kitchen, Empire Supper Club.

Royal Promenade

The main street.

A two-deck indoor avenue with shops, bars, casual dining, and the central atrium. The crossroads of the ship — you'll pass through it constantly.

AquaDome

Forward-facing showpiece.

A glass-domed venue at the front of the ship. Home of the AquaTheater (high-dive shows over a stage-pool) and the Overlook bar with floor-to-ceiling forward views.

Hideaway

Adults only — note for planning.

An 18+ neighborhood with its own infinity pool and DJ scene. Off-limits to your 4-year-old, but useful to know if grandparents or other relatives ever join a future trip.

Suite Neighborhood

Private suite-only zone.

A private area with its own pool, restaurant, and lounge — accessible only to suite-class guests. None of the four cabins quoted include this access.

For your 4-year-old

What's actually built for a small child

The kid-specific things worth knowing

Icon was designed with families in mind, and several features matter specifically for a 4-year-old:

  • Adventure Ocean (free until 10 PM): Royal Caribbean's complimentary kids program. On Icon specifically, the program is split into AO Babies, AO Juniors, and AO Kids — at age 4, your child slots into AO Juniors (3–5 years). Drop-off play, themed activities, supervised by trained staff. Available daily, port days included. Hourly fee applies after 10 PM if you want to keep them later.
  • Splashaway Bay (free): A kid-scaled water play area in Surfside with slides, fountains, and shallow splash zones. Designed for ages roughly 3–8.
  • Heads up — potty-training requirement: AO Juniors requires kids to be fully potty trained (no diapers or pull-ups). For most 4-year-olds this is a non-issue, but worth flagging. The Royal Babies & Tots Nursery (6mo–36mo) is the alternative for kids in diapers — your child has aged past that.
  • Surfside Eatery: Free, casual, all-day. Counter-service food a 4-year-old will actually eat — chicken tenders, pizza, fruit, ice cream. The single most useful dining option for parents on day one.
  • Pier 7 carousel & mini-golf: Free, included, in Surfside. Easy 30-minute breaks when you need a low-key activity.
  • Cabins matter for proximity: The Surfside Family Balcony option below puts you steps from all of the above. Worth flagging as you compare.
