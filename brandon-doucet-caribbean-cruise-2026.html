Reading Royal Caribbean's Cabin Categories

— a quick translator for the codes you're seeing above —

The number tells you the deck position, the letter tells you the cabin type

Royal Caribbean numbers cabin categories from best location (lower number) to less prime (higher number). The letter — D, B, J — is the cabin style. So a "2D" and a "4D" are the same physical room and balcony layout; the "2" just means it's on a more desirable deck.

2D and 4D = "Ocean View Balcony" (the standard balcony)

198 sq ft cabin + 46 sq ft balcony. King-size bed (or two twins), a sofa, full bathroom with shower. The classic Royal Caribbean balcony cabin — found on Decks 6 through 10. Some 4Ds on Deck 7 have lifeboats below that partially obstruct the view from the balcony, which is what brings the price down.

2B and 4B = "Spacious Ocean View Balcony" (slightly larger room, smaller balcony)

203 sq ft cabin + 42 sq ft balcony. Same basic layout as the standard balcony, but five extra square feet inside (four fewer on the balcony). Mostly a deck-position upgrade: 2Bs sit on better decks, 4Bs sit lower. The aft 4B on Deck 6 is a sleeper pick — same room as a 2B, with a wake view from the balcony.

J4 = "Junior Suite" (significantly bigger, with a tub)

277 sq ft cabin + 46 sq ft balcony. About 40% more interior space than a standard balcony, plus a real bathtub instead of a shower-only setup. Junior Suites get a few perks — priority boarding, dedicated suite breakfast — but not the full Suite Class lounge access (those come with Grand Suite categories and up). Found only on Decks 9 and 10.
