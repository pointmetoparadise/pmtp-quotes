Default Itinerary

Five Sights, One City

The standard route if no add-ons are selected. Your guide will pace the day around traffic and your energy.
1

Tsukiji Outer Market

Food street, post-wholesale era

The famous tuna auction moved to Toyosu in 2018, but the outer market kept running — narrow lanes of sushi counters, tamagoyaki stalls, dashi vendors, and knife shops. A good first stop for breakfast-style bites and a real sense of how Tokyo eats before the rest of the city wakes up.

2

Imperial Palace East Gardens

What remains of Edo Castle

The public-access gardens on the former inner grounds of Edo Castle, seat of the Tokugawa shogunate. Stone foundations of the old keep, seasonal plantings, and quiet paths in the middle of a city that otherwise doesn't pause. A working contrast to Tsukiji.

3

Tokyo Tower

333 meters, Shiba district, 1958

Orange-and-white steel, Eiffel-inspired, still a working broadcast tower. Tokyo Tower is the older icon — less tall than the Skytree but more affectionately loved, and from here you get a classic mid-city vantage over Roppongi, Shiba Park, and the bay.

4

Shibuya Crossing

The scramble

Up to 3,000 people moving across five crosswalks in a single light cycle. Your guide will position you for the best street-level view, and if the timing works, you'll cross it yourselves. The Hachiko statue is a few steps away.

5

Shibuya Sky

Open-air rooftop, 229 meters up

The rooftop observation deck atop Shibuya Scramble Square — no glass overhead, full 360° view, and on a clear day, Mt. Fuji off to the southwest. Best late-afternoon into sunset when the light turns.
